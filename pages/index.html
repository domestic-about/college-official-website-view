College Introduction

Huazhong Agricultural University Animal Science and Technology College

The College of Animal Science and Technology and the College of Veterinary Medicine are among the earliest established faculties at Huazhong Agricultural University. In 1898, under the leadership of Governor-General Zhang Zhidong, the Hubei Provincial Agricultural School (predecessor of Huazhong Agricultural University) was founded with the aim of attracting aspiring individuals interested in agricultural studies and research on crop cultivation and animal husbandry. In 1902, it was renamed as Hubei Provincial Higher Agricultural School and began offering programs in agriculture, animal husbandry, and forestry. In 1916, the school split into two, and Hubei Provincial Second Higher Agricultural School established a three-year program in veterinary medicine. In 1952, as part of the nationwide restructuring of institutions, the Department of Animal Husbandry and Veterinary Medicine was established within Huazhong Agricultural College, with Professor Qin Liran, a renowned veterinarian, serving as its first department head. In October 1995, the College of Animal Science and Veterinary Medicine was established at Huazhong Agricultural University, which was later renamed as the College of Animal Science and Technology and the College of Veterinary Medicine in July 2002.

RESEARCH

Our college currently houses 6 national-level research institutions, 1 collaborative innovation center, 3 provincial-level international science and technology cooperation bases, and 18 provincial and ministerial-level research institutions. Additionally, we have been approved to establish a national experimental teaching demonstration center for veterinary medicine, an education and agriculture cooperation talent training base for pig farming in collaboration with the Ministry of Education and the Ministry of Agriculture, as well as an innovation activity base for undergraduate students majoring in animal science and technology in Hubei Province.

Research Achievements

The achievements of the college

The research team has made significant progress in recent studies, achieving new research outcomes.

Our Animal Genetics and Breeding Team has launched the IAnimal, a cross-species multi-omics knowledgebase.

The research team led by Professor Zhao Shuhong has published a research paper titled "IAnimal: A cross-species omics knowledgebase for animals" in the journal "Nucleic Acids Research".

We have made progress in the research on the functional role of microbiota-derived metabolites in the intestinal tract of pigs at our institute.

Recently, a research paper titled "Gut microbiota-derived 3-phenylpropionic acid promotes intestinal epithelial barrier function via AhR signaling" was published in the journal Microbiome by the National Key Laboratory of Agricultural Microbial Resources Exploration and Utilization, the Ministry of Education's Frontier Science Center for Animal Breeding and Healthy Farming, and the Yan Xianghua Research Group at the Hubei Hongshan Laboratory.

The college has made new progress in the understanding of the pathogenic mechanism of African swine fever virus and the development of vaccines.

The research article titled "African swine fever virus I73R is a critical virulence-related gene: A potential target for attenuation," authored by Professor Peng Guiqing's team from our college in collaboration with Professor Chen Hongjun's team from the Shanghai Veterinary Research Institute, Chinese Academy of Agricultural Sciences, has been published online in the international academic journal Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences. The study found that the recombinant virus lacking the I73R gene completely lost its pathogenicity to domestic pigs and exhibited resistance to the lethal dose of the parental virulent strain, indicating that I73R is one of the major virulence genes encoded by ASFV.

The Animal Genetics and Breeding team at the college has released a new computational tool called HIBLUP for genomic breeding utilizing big data.

The research article titled "HIBLUP: An integration of statistical models on the BLUP framework for efficient genetic evaluation using big genomic data" by Professor Zhao Shuhong's team at the college has been published online in the journal "Nucleic Acids Research."

Professor Li Jiakui's team at our college has made new progress in the study of tibial dyschondroplasia in broiler chickens.

Professor Li Jiakui's team at our college has published a research paper titled "Thiram-induced hyperglycemia causes tibial dyschondroplasia by triggering aberrant ECM remodeling via the gut-pancreas axis in broiler chickens" in the Journal of Hazardous Materials.

Our college has made significant progress in the research and application of beneficial microbial strains in yak breeding.

The research team led by Professor Li Jiakui from our college has made significant progress in the development and application of beneficial microbial strains derived from yaks. Their research paper was published in the international microbiology journal Microbiology Spectrum.

Zhao Shuhong

Dr. Zhao Shuhong is a professor and doctoral supervisor at Huazhong Agricultural University, holding a second-level position. He is also a visiting professor at Murdoch University in Australia. He is a member of the 7th and 8th Discipline Evaluation Committees (Animal Husbandry) of the Academic Degrees Committee of the State Council. He serves as a member of the 8th Advisory Committee of the Department of Life Sciences of the National Natural Science Foundation of China. Dr. Zhao is a scientist in the National Pig System Whole Genome Selection Position, as well as a Tang Scholar at Cornell University in the United States. Currently, Dr. Zhao is the Dean of the College of Animal Science and Technology and the College of Veterinary Medicine at Huazhong Agricultural University. He also serves as the Director of the National Livestock Engineering Technology Research Center, the Director of the Key Laboratory of Agricultural Animal Genetics, Breeding, and Reproduction of the Ministry of Education, and the Executive Deputy Director of the Supervision, Inspection, and Testing Center for Swine Quality of the Ministry of Agriculture (Wuhan). Dr. Zhao has held various important positions, including Co-Chair of the International Symposium on Animal and Plant Genomes (2009-2011), member of the organizing committee of the Asian Symposium on Animal and Plant Genomes, Executive Council member and Vice Chairman of the Animal Genetics and Breeding Society of China, and Executive Committee member of the International Society for Animal Genetics Pig Genome Committee, among others.

Dr. Zhao Shuhong's main research focus is on pig genomics and breeding. In response to the major needs of basic research and industry in the Chinese pig sector, he has deciphered the regulatory mechanisms of important economic traits in pigs. He has developed a series of omics methods and tools, created an integrated omics gene mining technology system for pigs, and developed a number of molecular breeding markers with independent intellectual property rights. He has also established a pig genomics breeding technology system and successfully bred and created several lean meat pig lines, high-quality black pigs, and genetically edited breeding materials. His achievements in the field of pig omics technology and germplasm innovation are outstanding. Dr. Zhao has undertaken more than 20 projects, including the Key Program of the National High Technology Research and Development Program (863 Program), major projects of the National Natural Science Foundation of China, major international cooperation projects, and the 973 Program. He has published over 200 papers in journals such as Nature Biotechnology and Nature Communications. He has been granted more than 60 national invention patents and has received numerous scientific and technological awards, including the National Technical Invention Second Prize, the Ministry of Education Science and Technology Progress First Prize, and the Hubei Provincial Technical Invention First Prize. He has also been honored with the Chinese Young Women Scientist Award, the Chinese Youth Science and Technology Award, the National March 8th Red Flag Bearer, the Advanced Individual in Ethical Education in Hubei Province, and the Outstanding Communist Party Member in Hubei Province. Dr. Zhao has led his team to be selected as an innovative team in the field of pig genomics and germplasm innovation by the Ministry of Education, an outstanding talent in agricultural scientific research by the Ministry of Agriculture, and an innovative team of leading talents by the Ministry of Science and Technology. He has served as an editorial board member for more than 10 journals, including China Science - Life Sciences, FASE, PLOS ONE, Frontiers in Genetics, and Chinese Agricultural Science. He has trained and provided a large number of technical backbone and senior management talents for universities, research institutes, and leading agricultural and animal husbandry enterprises nationwide
