Department of Animal Genetics and Breeding

The Department of Animal Genetics and Breeding focuses on the research and study of animal genetics and breeding. Our department explores various aspects related to the genetic makeup and breeding practices of animals. We aim to improve animal traits, such as productivity, disease resistance, and adaptability, through selective breeding and genetic enhancement techniques.

Our research areas include:
  • 1. Genetic Diversity and Conservation: We investigate the genetic diversity of animal populations and develop strategies for their conservation and sustainable utilization.
  • 2. Breeding Strategies and Selection: We develop breeding strategies and implement selection programs to enhance desired traits in animals, such as growth rate, milk production, meat quality, and disease resistance.
  • 3. Molecular Genetics and Genomics: We utilize molecular genetic techniques and genomics to unravel the genetic basis of complex traits in animals and identify candidate genes associated with important traits.
  • 4. Reproductive Technologies: We explore reproductive technologies, including artificial insemination, embryo transfer, in vitro fertilization, and cloning, to improve breeding efficiency and preserve valuable genetic resources.
  • 5. Genomic Selection and Marker-Assisted Breeding: We employ advanced genomic tools and techniques to accelerate the selection process, predict genetic merit, and facilitate marker-assisted breeding in animals.
  • 6. Genetic Improvement for Adaptation: We study the genetic mechanisms underlying animal adaptation to different environments, including heat stress, disease challenges, and nutritional constraints, with the goal of developing resilient and adaptable animal populations.

Through our research endeavors, we aim to contribute to the sustainable development of animal agriculture, the conservation of genetic resources, and the improvement of animal welfare and productivity.

The Department of Animal Nutrition and Feed Science

The Department of Animal Nutrition and Feed Science is dedicated to researching the nutritional requirements of animals and advancing the science of animal feed. Our department aims to optimize animal feeding practices and feed formulations, ultimately enhancing animal production performance and overall health

Our research areas include:
  • 1. Feed Composition and Nutrition: Investigating the effects of different feed ingredients on animal nutrition requirements and physiological functions. This includes studying the absorption, utilization, and metabolism of nutrients such as energy, protein, vitamins, minerals, and other essential components.
  • 2. Feed Additives and Functional Feeds: Exploring the application of additives and functional feeds, such as enzymes, probiotics, antioxidants, and other substances that enhance animal health and performance.
  • 3. Feed Quality and Safety: Assessing feed quality, including factors such as nutrient content, digestibility, and potential contaminants. We strive to ensure that animal feed meets the necessary standards for optimal animal health and performance.
  • 4. Feed Formulation and Ration Balancing: Developing scientifically balanced feed formulations that meet the specific nutritional requirements of different animal species and production stages.
  • 5. Feed Technology and Processing: Investigating innovative feed processing techniques to improve feed quality, digestibility, and palatability, thereby enhancing animal feed intake and utilization.

Our department collaborates with industry partners, academic institutions, and research organizations to contribute to the advancement of animal nutrition and feed science. By conducting research, providing education and training, and offering consultancy services, we aim to support the sustainable and efficient production of high-quality animal products.

Department of Animal Breeding and Special Animal Economic Zoology

The Department of Animal Reproduction and Special Animal Economic Zoology is dedicated to the study of animal reproduction and the economic aspects of special animal species.

Our department focuses on conducting research and providing education in the field of animal breeding, encompassing areas such as reproductive physiology, genetics, and breeding techniques. Our primary goal is to enhance the genetic potential and overall productivity of various animal species, including livestock, poultry, and aquaculture.

In addition to animal breeding, we specialize in the economic aspects of special animal species. This includes analyzing their market value, exploring different production systems, and assessing the economic viability of raising these animals. Our research endeavors aim to understand the unique characteristics and specific requirements of these animals, while identifying opportunities for sustainable economic growth and development within the industry.

Through collaborations with industry partners, governmental organizations, and research institutions, we actively work towards developing innovative strategies and technologies that contribute to the efficient and responsible management of special animal species. Our department is committed to promoting economic growth, ensuring environmental sustainability, and prioritizing the welfare of these animals, while also addressing the needs of the agricultural and livestock sectors.

We strive to provide a comprehensive education and research platform for students and professionals interested in the field of animal reproduction and the economic aspects of special animal species. Our faculty members are experienced and dedicated to mentoring the next generation of scientists and professionals in this specialized area of study.

The Department of Intelligent Animal Farming

At our department, we focus on integrating cutting-edge technologies, such as artificial intelligence, big data analytics, Internet of Things (IoT), and robotics, into animal farming practices. Our research and educational programs aim to develop innovative solutions for optimizing livestock production, improving resource management, and ensuring the well-being of animals.

Through the application of intelligent farming systems, we strive to enhance the monitoring, control, and management of animal environments, including temperature, humidity, ventilation, and feeding. We utilize sensors, data analytics, and automation to create smart farming systems that enable real-time monitoring, predictive modeling, and decision support for farmers.

Our department collaborates with industry partners, technology providers, and agricultural organizations to explore new approaches in precision farming, smart feeding, disease detection and prevention, and automated husbandry practices. We aim to create sustainable and efficient farming systems that optimize resource utilization, minimize environmental impact, and promote animal health and welfare.

By providing interdisciplinary education and training, our department equips students with the knowledge and skills necessary to implement intelligent farming practices. Students gain expertise in data analysis, automation technologies, and animal behavior monitoring, preparing them for careers in precision agriculture, agricultural technology development, and farm management.

The Department of Intelligent Animal Farming is at the forefront of research and innovation in the application of intelligent technologies to animal farming. We are committed to advancing the field of intelligent animal farming and contributing to the development of sustainable and technology-driven agriculture.

The Department of Basic Veterinary Medicine

The Department of Basic Veterinary Medicine is dedicated to providing foundational knowledge and skills in veterinary science. Our curriculum focuses on fundamental concepts and principles that form the basis of veterinary medicine. Through a combination of theoretical instruction and practical training, students gain a comprehensive understanding of anatomy, physiology, pathology, pharmacology, and other essential disciplines.

Our department is committed to fostering a strong theoretical foundation in veterinary medicine, which serves as the cornerstone for further specialization and advanced studies. We emphasize hands-on learning experiences and laboratory exercises to develop students' practical skills and enhance their ability to diagnose and treat animal diseases.

With a team of experienced faculty members and state-of-the-art facilities, we strive to cultivate competent and compassionate veterinary professionals. Our graduates are well-equipped to contribute to the health and welfare of animals, serving as a vital link between animal owners, veterinarians, and the broader community.

Through our rigorous academic programs and research initiatives, the Department of Basic Veterinary Medicine aims to advance veterinary knowledge, improve animal health outcomes, and promote the overall well-being of animals.

Preventive Veterinary Studies

Preventive Veterinary Medicine, also known as Veterinary Preventive Medicine, is a field of study that focuses on the prevention and control of diseases in animals. It involves various strategies and practices aimed at maintaining the health and well-being of animals and minimizing the spread of infectious diseases.

In the field of Preventive Veterinary Medicine, students learn about disease surveillance, risk assessment, biosecurity measures, vaccination programs, and other preventive measures. The curriculum includes courses on epidemiology, public health, zoonotic diseases, disease control strategies, and veterinary diagnostics.

Through practical training and hands-on experiences, students acquire the necessary skills to identify potential disease outbreaks, implement preventive measures, and collaborate with other professionals to protect animal populations. They also learn how to educate animal owners and the public about disease prevention and promote responsible animal healthcare practices.

Preventive Veterinary Medicine plays a vital role in safeguarding animal health, improving animal welfare, and minimizing the transmission of diseases from animals to humans. Graduates in this field can pursue careers in veterinary clinics, public health agencies, research institutions, government organizations, and international health organizations, working towards the prevention and control of diseases in animals and ensuring the health of both animals and humans.

The Department of Clinical Veterinary Medicine

The Department of Clinical Veterinary Medicine is dedicated to the study and practice of veterinary medicine in a clinical setting. Our department focuses on providing comprehensive medical care for animals, diagnosing and treating various diseases and injuries, and promoting animal health and welfare.

Our faculty consists of experienced veterinarians and experts in specialized fields, who are committed to training the next generation of veterinary professionals. Through a combination of classroom instruction, laboratory work, and practical clinical experiences, students in our department gain a solid foundation in veterinary medicine and develop essential clinical skills.

Our department offers a wide range of courses and training programs that cover various aspects of clinical veterinary medicine, including animal examination and diagnosis, medical imaging, surgery, anesthesia, emergency care, and preventive medicine. Students have the opportunity to work closely with faculty members and experienced veterinarians,

participating in real-world clinical cases and gaining hands-on experience in veterinary care.

We also actively engage in research activities to advance the field of clinical veterinary medicine. Our faculty and students conduct research on innovative treatment methods, disease prevention strategies, and advancements in diagnostic techniques. Through our research endeavors, we strive to improve the health and well-being of animals and contribute to the overall development of veterinary medicine.

The Department of Clinical Veterinary Medicine is committed to excellence in education, research, and clinical practice. We aim to produce competent and compassionate veterinarians who are capable of providing high-quality healthcare to animals and making a positive impact in the field of veterinary medicine.

The Department of Veterinary Pharmacology and Toxicology

The Department of Veterinary Pharmacology and Toxicology focuses on the study of veterinary drugs and their effects on animals. Our department aims to understand the mechanisms of action, pharmacokinetics, and pharmacodynamics of various veterinary drugs, as well as their safety and potential adverse effects.

We conduct research on the development and evaluation of new veterinary drugs, including their formulation, dosage, and efficacy. We also investigate drug interactions, drug resistance, and the impact of environmental factors on drug metabolism in animals.

Our department offers a wide range of courses and training programs that cover various aspects of clinical veterinary medicine, including animal examination and diagnosis, medical imaging, surgery, anesthesia, emergency care, and preventive medicine. Students have the opportunity to work closely with faculty members and experienced veterinarians,

In addition to drug research, our department is involved in toxicology studies, evaluating the safety and toxicity of various substances that may be encountered by animals in their environment or through veterinary treatments. We strive to identify potential risks and develop strategies for minimizing toxicity and ensuring the safe use of veterinary drugs.

Through our research and teaching activities, we aim to contribute to the advancement of veterinary medicine, promote animal health and welfare, and provide valuable insights and guidance for veterinarians, researchers, and the veterinary pharmaceutical industry.

The Department of Public Health

The Department of Public Health focuses on the study of population health and the prevention of diseases and injuries in communities. Our department is dedicated to improving the overall health and well-being of the public through research, education, and the development of public health policies and interventions.

We conduct studies and research to understand the determinants of health and disease in populations, including social, behavioral, environmental, and biological factors. Our goal is to identify risk factors, patterns of disease occurrence, and health disparities, and to develop evidence-based strategies for disease prevention, health promotion, and health protection

Our department offers education and training in public health principles and practices, equipping students with the knowledge and skills to address public health challenges. We collaborate with governmental and non-governmental organizations, healthcare providers, and community stakeholders to implement public health programs and interventions aimed at improving population health outcomes.

Areas of focus within the Department of Public Health include epidemiology, biostatistics, environmental health, occupational health, health policy and management, health promotion, and disease surveillance. Through our multidisciplinary approach, we strive to advance public health knowledge, contribute to the development of effective public health policies, and make a positive impact on the health of individuals and communities.
